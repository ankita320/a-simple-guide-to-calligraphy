Before getting to the actual calligraphy, let's quickly go over what type of pens could work. Use something that is flexible enough and pointy at the tip of the pen. Here are some examples!

Holding the pen properly is really important when learning calligraphy. You want to make sure it's around a 45 degree angle. Obviously, this doens't mean to go and measure it but it just to simply eyeball it and hold the pen in your hand at around that angle. This plays a big role in your calligraphy because you dont want to hold it stright upwards or bent completely downwards as it would not create proper up or down strokes. You want to keep it somewhere around the middle so you are able to accurately do up and down strokes without many, difficulties. Remember to hold in your hand the way you are comfortable with it being that is still around a 45 degree angle. You don't have to hold it EXACTLY as you see someone else holding it but rather in a way that is easier for YOU.
